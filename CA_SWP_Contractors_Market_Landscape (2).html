The State Water Project

The California State Water Project (SWP) is the nation's largest state-built water storage and conveyance system — a 705-mile network of canals, pipelines, reservoirs, and hydroelectric facilities managed by the California Department of Water Resources (DWR). The 29 long-term water supply contractors signed contracts with DWR in the 1960s, each defining a maximum annual entitlement (the "Table A" amount). Actual deliveries vary annually: the 2026 allocation is 30% of Table A; the long-term average is ~60%; critically dry years can see 0–5%. Original contracts expired in 2035; DWR Director approved extension to 2085 in December 2018. Five contractors use SWP water primarily for agricultural purposes; 24 primarily for municipal and industrial use.

Delta Conveyance Project (DCP) — The Defining Capital Challenge

DWR's proposed twin-tunnel project to convey Sacramento River water beneath the Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta, bypassing fragile levees vulnerable to earthquake collapse. Would significantly improve supply reliability for all 29 SWP contractors. Cost: $16B+. Full environmental review ongoing; Draft EIR completed. Supported by most municipal SWP contractors. This is the single most consequential capital and policy issue affecting the entire SWP contractor community — its cost-sharing will affect all 29 agencies' finances for decades.

Contract Extension to 2085 & Financial Framework

Original SWP contracts expired in 2035. DWR Director approved Water Supply Contract Extension Amendment in December 2018 (effective January 1, 2024), extending contracts to 2085. This allows DWR to issue 30-year+ revenue bonds to finance SWP capital improvements — critical for financing the Delta Conveyance Project and ongoing infrastructure needs. All 29 contractors pay all SWP capital and operating costs proportionally based on their Table A amounts. Contractors' SWP payments are a significant operating cost line item for many agencies.

Note on Financial Data

Revenue and budget data varies significantly in availability across the 29 contractors. Large agencies (MWD, Valley Water, CVWD, Zone 7) publish detailed ACFRs with audited financials. Mid-size agencies vary — many publish ACFRs but exact figures require manual extraction. Small agricultural districts (Dudley Ridge, Oak Flat, Empire West Side, Tulare Lake Basin) have very limited public financial disclosure. Where exact audited figures are confirmed from official sources, confidence is noted as HIGH. Budget estimates are noted as MEDIUM. Third-party estimates only are noted as LOW. No figures are generated without a cited source.

2026 Allocation Context

The 2026 SWP allocation is 30% of Table A (NTC 26-01, January 2026). At 30%, total SWP deliveries to all 29 contractors are approximately 1.25 million acre-feet for 2026. The long-term average is ~60% (~2.5M AFY). 2006 was the last 100% allocation year. The 2025 allocation was 50%. Contractors supplement SWP supplies with local groundwater, recycled water, surface water, water banking, and transfers to manage shortfalls. All 29 contractors budget and plan around multi-year supply uncertainty — many maintain 1–3+ years of storage reserves.

Receives SWP exchange water via Salton Sea Exchange for groundwater replenishment. The long-planned Coachella Canal connector tunnel remains cost-prohibitive. Source: cvwd.org
