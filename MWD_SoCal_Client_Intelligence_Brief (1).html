Who They Are

The Metropolitan Water District of Southern California (Metropolitan, or Met) is the largest treated water wholesaler in the United States — a state-chartered public agency created by California state law in 1928. Metropolitan provides its 26 member agencies with water for nearly 19 million people across six Southern California counties: Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino, San Diego, and Ventura. It imports water from the Colorado River via the 242-mile Colorado River Aqueduct (CRA, built 1933–1941) and from Northern California via the State Water Project (SWP). Metropolitan operates 5 water treatment plants (among the largest in the nation), 20 reservoirs, 24 dams, 830 miles of pipelines, and 14 CRA pumping plants across a 5,200-square-mile service area. It is governed by a 38-member Board of Directors representing its 26 member agencies.

In October 2025, Shivaji Deshmukh was appointed General Manager-designate (effective January 1, 2026), replacing retiring GM Deven Upadhyay. Deshmukh came from Inland Empire Utilities Agency — one of Metropolitan's own 26 member agencies — where he was GM for six years, and previously served as assistant GM at West Basin Municipal Water District. He brings a member agency perspective to the GM role.

Post-2026 Colorado River Operating Guidelines — Supply Uncertainty
CRITICAL
Metropolitan's Colorado River operations run under shortage guidelines expiring end of 2026. Bureau of Reclamation DEIS process for post-2026 guidelines is underway. Metropolitan is the largest Colorado River user in Southern California — a lead stakeholder alongside CAP/CAWCD, SNWA, and LADWP. The post-2026 framework will determine Metropolitan's supply certainty, rate trajectory, and capital program priorities for the next 20+ years. CAMP4W was designed specifically to manage this supply uncertainty. The 2026 SWP allocation is also 30% of Table A, further compressing near-term supply.
Source: 2026 Annual Operating Plan; MWD Biennial Budget Book; Bureau of Reclamation DEIS process.
